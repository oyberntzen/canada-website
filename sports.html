Introduction

Sports are a big part of Canadian culture. The most popular sports in Canada are ice hockey, lacrosse, gridiron football, soccer, basketball, curling and baseball. Ice hockey is the official winter sport and is probably the most famous Canadian sport as it started in Canada. Lacrosse is the official summer sport. More details on ice hockey and lacrosse are given in the sections below. In addition, there are many other popular sports in Canada, and tons of famous Canadian sport athletes. Some of the all-time most famous Canadian sport athletes are wrestler Roddy Piper, ice hockey player Wayne Gretzky, wrestler Bret Hart, basketball player Steve Nash, and basketball player Andrew Wiggins. When visiting Canada, you should definitely see a sports event, even if you are not interested in sports.

National Sports

Ice Hockey

Ice hockey started in Canada in the late 19th century. The sport is played on ice with skates, and the goal of the game is to shoot the puck into the net of the other team. Each team has six players and one of them is the keeper. The game is split up into three periods lasting twenty minutes each. The team with the most goals wins, and if it is a draw, the game will either end with no winner or continue until a team gets a goal.

Lacrosse

Lacrosse is a sport first played by the indigenous people in North America. The sport was modified by the colonizers, and it became lacrosse as we know it today. There are 10 players on each team in lacrosse. Each person has a stick with a small net on the end. Multisport Events

A multisport event is an event with multiple sports. Two of the multisport events that have taken place in Canada are Canada Games and Olympic Games.

Canada Games

Canada Games is held every two years, alternating between Canada Summer Games and Canada Winter Games. Canada Games is only for young athletes. Each athlete represents their province or territory. All the all the sports for Canada Summer Games are listed here, and all the sports for Canada Winter Games are listed here.

Olympic Games

Canada has competed at almost every Olympic Games. At the Winter Olympic Games, Canada is often one of the nations winning the most medals. Unfortunately, Canada is not doing as well in the Summer Olympic Games. Canada has hosted the Olympic Games three times in 1976, 1988 and 2010. We do not know yet when Canada will host their next Olympic Games, but with some luck and planning you might catch the event.
