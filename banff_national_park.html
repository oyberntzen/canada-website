Info About the Banff National Park

Banff National Park is the first established national park in Canada. The size of Banff National Park is huge, covering over 6.5 thousand square kilometers. Furthermore, the terrain is very varied and consists of mountains, dense forests, lakes, glaciers, and ice fields. There is also a great variety of animals in the park, with 56 different mammal species being recorded including bears, cougars, wolverines, and beavers. In other words, the nature in Banff National Park is very diverse and unique. For this reason, the national park has more than three million annual visitors. This makes Banff National Park one of the most visited national parks in North America, and tourists visit the national park in both the summer and the winter. Some examples of activities in Banff National Park in the winter are cross-country skiing, alpine, snowboarding, ice climbing, and much more. Some examples of activities in the summer are hiking, biking, swimming, fishing, running and much more. The next section will cover some recommended hikes in Banff National Park.

Recommended Hikes in Banff National Park

  1. Lake Agnes Teahouse
    Difficulty: easy
    Distance: 7.2 km
    Elevation gain: 390 m
    Time required: 3-4 hours

  2. Cascade Amphitheatre
    Difficulty: moderate
    Distance: 13.2 km
    Elevation gain: 640 m
    Time required: 4-6 hours

  3. Cory Pass
    Difficulty: challenging
    Distance: 13 km
    Elevation gain: 1000 m
    Time required: 5-6 hours
